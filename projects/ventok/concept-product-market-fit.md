# product-market fit

The core thesis: manufacturers in 5-50 person range have acute Excel pain and will pay $200-500/mo for safety, not features.

## validation: Noar (CFO, wood manufacturing, ~15 people)

### the pain
- **order reconciliation**: 6 hours per week (30% of ops time) spent matching customer orders to invoices manually
- **quoting process**: manually building cost sheets in Excel (2-3 hours per quote, slow turnaround loses deals)
- **process visibility**: no way to see what's in progress; teams ask "where's order X?" 20x/day
- **scaling friction**: hired new ops person but couldn't hand them systems (everything is "ask Ron" or "look at spreadsheet")

### why they signed at $72/mo
- **time-to-payback**: 2 weeks (saves 5hr/week at $20/hr ops cost = $400/mo value)
- **safety**: Noar's CFO said "I trust you won't break our process. Most tools try to change how we work." (she picked reliability over features)
- **domain understanding**: we spoke their language (wood pricing, order types, invoice cycles), not generic workflow terms

### what we learned
1. **reliability beats features**: they don't want "AI" or "advanced workflows", they want "won't lose my data"
2. **pain specificity matters**: "inefficiency" doesn't sell. "6 hours per week" does.
3. **founder-customer relationship works**: they signed because they trust us, not because they audited competitors
4. **SMB cash flow is real**: $72/mo is premium enough to feel expensive, cheap enough to not need approval

## the hypothesis

**target:** manufacturers 5-50 people, using Excel for order/quote/reconciliation

**pain:** manual processes waste 5-10 hours/week per ops person

**solution:** automated workflows + visibility (not AI-first, but safe + fast)

**pricing power:** payback period <3 weeks means we can charge $200-500/mo

**market size:** estimated 800+ companies in Estonia/EU (unvalidated)

**viability threshold:** 10-15 customers at $200+/mo = $2-3k/mo (sustainable operations) → 30+ customers = $6-15k/mo (growth phase)

---

## gaps in validation

- **retention**: 1 customer isn't enough to know if they stay
- **churn drivers**: unknown (price? product changes? switching costs?)
- **expansion**: do customers upgrade from Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3?
- **vertical variation**: does pain look the same in furniture vs food processing vs pharma?
- **geographic expansion**: do manufacturers in Germany/Sweden/Poland have same pain?
