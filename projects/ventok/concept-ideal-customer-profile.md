# ideal customer profile

**quick summary:** 5-50 person manufacturing company where Excel pain is acute enough to justify budget. Decision-maker has operational responsibility (CFO, ops manager, founder).

---

## firmographic fit

| criterion | fit | example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **company size** | 5-50 people | Noar (15p), TMW (est. 20p), Luminor (est. 30p) |
| **revenue range** | €200k-€2M annual | implies 2-4 ops staff who touch Excel daily |
| **industry** | manufacturing (primary) | wood, metal fabrication, food processing, assembly |
| **location** | Estonia/Nordics/EU | timezone overlap, familiar regulations, cold outreach works |
| **maturity** | 3+ years (established) | they have repeatable processes worth automating |

---

## buying signals (ask in research, listen for in calls)

**hot signal (talk to today):**
- recently hired ops person ("we need to document what we do")
- CTO/CFO mentions scaling friction ("we can't hire faster, Excel isn't keeping up")
- lost deal due to slow response ("took 3 days to build quote")
- manual invoice/order reconciliation visible on their website or blog

**warm signal (talk to this month):**
- publicly expanding (new product line, new market)
- process-heavy description of operations (mentions quoting, scheduling, tracking)
- tech-adjacent language but no software solution named ("we're looking for tools")

**cold signal (deprioritize):**
- recently bought competing software (Airtable, SAP, industry-specific tool)
- explicit statement "we have a system for this"
- company <5 people (single founder can't justify purchase)

---

## disqualifiers (skip this prospect)

- "We use SAP / NetSuite / [enterprise ERP]" → above our positioning
- "We outsource ops entirely" → no internal pain owner
- "100+ employees" → will want custom implementation, SaaS doesn't fit
- "We already digitalized" + specificity → they solved the pain (unless it's the wrong solution)

---

## decision-maker profile

**who signs:**
- CFO / Finance manager (controls budget, feels reconciliation pain)
- Operations manager (feels workflow pain, quality pain)
- Founder/Owner (bootstrapped, feels all pain, controls 5-50p co)

**who doesn't (skip them):**
- IT director alone (will overthink / want custom dev)
- admin staff (can't approve purchase)
- consultant hired for "digital transformation" (will recommend big vendors)

---

## qualification questions (for discovery calls)

1. **pain specificity**: "Which of these takes your team 5+ hours per week?" (quote building, reconciliation, order tracking, scheduling)
2. **process maturity**: "Walk me through your current quoting process" (if it's all in their head → too early; if it's documented in Excel → perfect fit)
3. **buying power**: "Who would need to approve a $200/mo tool?" (if it's >3 approvals → deprioritize)
4. **timeline**: "If we could save you 10 hours per week, when would you want to start?" (answer reveals urgency)
5. **trust**: "What would need to happen for you to trust an external system with your order data?" (listen for risk sensitivity)
