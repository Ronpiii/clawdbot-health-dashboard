# competitive landscape

The core insight: no one owns "safe automation for small manufacturers." Everything else is either too cheap (fails on reliability) or too expensive (fails on SMB budgets).

---

## direct competitors (we're fighting these in sales)

### airtable
- **what:** low-code automation platform (any workflow)
- **price:** €0-240+/mo
- **target:** generalists, small teams
- **strength:** low cost, flexible
- **vs ventok:**
  - ❌ no manufacturing domain knowledge (quotes are "records", not real workflows)
  - ❌ high implementation friction (requires deep customization)
  - ❌ support load (customers hit walls, need expert help)
  - ❌ reliability anxiety (feels fragile, not "tested by manufacturers")
  - ✅ cheaper upfront
  
**what we say:** "Airtable is great for generic workflows, but manufacturing has real-world constraints (pricing logic, order dependencies, invoice reconciliation). We handle those out-of-the-box, so you don't need a consultant."

### zapier / make (former integromat)
- **what:** automation glue (if X, then Y)
- **price:** €10-500+/mo
- **target:** process owners, non-technical
- **strength:** lots of integrations
- **vs ventok:**
  - ❌ fragile (breaks when tools change APIs)
  - ❌ no manufacturing logic (you're just piping data)
  - ❌ expensive for complex workflows (task-based pricing kills ROI)
  - ❌ support? their answer is "read docs" (no domain help)
  - ✅ quick for simple things
  
**what we say:** "Zapier connects tools, but doesn't understand your business. We embed manufacturing logic—pricing rules, order dependencies, validation—so your workflow is smart, not just automated."

### industry-specific software (Planisware, industry ERPs, etc.)
- **what:** all-in-one manufacturing software
- **price:** €2k-20k+/mo or custom
- **target:** enterprises, mid-market
- **strength:** comprehensive, audited, enterprise-grade
- **vs ventok:**
  - ❌ way too expensive for 5-50p companies
  - ❌ 3-6 month implementation (SMB cash flow can't wait)
  - ❌ bloated (you pay for CRM, AP, HR even if you just need order automation)
  - ❌ change management burden (everyone has to learn new system)
  - ✅ thorough, integrated, safe
  
**what we say:** "Enterprise software is built for big orgs. We're built for fast growth. You get manufacturing intelligence without the implementation tax."

## adjacent competitors (they might do this, so we could lose)

### generic B2B SaaS that adds manufacturing features
- **examples:** accounting software (Xero, FreshBooks) adding manufacturing
- **risk:** they have distribution, brand trust, can lower prices
- **vs ventok:** they see us as feature creep, not core offering
- **defense:** move upmarket faster (Tier 2/3 offerings), build relationships so switching costs are high

### large consulting firms offering automation
- **examples:** Deloitte Digital, Accenture offering "custom automation"
- **risk:** 0 (they don't touch sub-€1M company segment)
- **advantage to us:** manufacturers comparing us to consultants actually prefer us (faster, cheaper, ongoing support vs one-shot engagement)

---

## non-competitors (they're solving different pain)

- **SAP, NetSuite, Oracle:** enterprise resource planning (wrong segment)
- **Shopify, Square:** ecommerce (wrong workflow)
- **Monday.com, Asana:** project management (wrong use case)
- **Excel add-ons, VBA consultants:** they're our *feeder* (when Excel gets too painful, our TAM grows)

## why we win sales

1. **they tried Airtable, it broke**: we show them how to do it right
2. **they're scared of big enterprise software**: we show them fast, affordable alternative
3. **they don't trust consultants anymore**: we're founder-to-founder trust, not vendor-to-customer
4. **they have specific pain**: we speak their language (quoting, reconciliation, order tracking)

---

## how competitors could kill us

1. **Airtable ships manufacturing templates** (unlikely, not their focus)
2. **a manufacturing software company pivots to SMB** (possible, but they'd need to rebuild their GTM)
3. **consultants get better at automation** (possible but low probability, they're incentivized to be slow)
