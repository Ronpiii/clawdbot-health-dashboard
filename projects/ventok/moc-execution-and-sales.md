### discovery & sales
- [[discovery-call-script]] — how to qualify, find pain, position solution
  - qualification: "which of these takes you 5+ hours per week?" → maps to product tier
  - pain mapping: move from generic ("we're inefficient") to specific ("reconciliation takes 6hr/week")
  - objection handling: "why not [competitor]?" → safety + domain + speed answers

- [[service-tiers]] — what you're actually selling
  - tier 1: infrastructure (domain setup, process mapping, initial automation)
  - tier 2: automation (workflow design, integration, training)
  - tier 3: AI workflows (personal assistant, predictive analytics)

---

## decision rules (apply during calls)

1. **paint before you propose**: let them describe the pain first, then show how you fix it
2. **reliability > features**: emphasize "we don't break your process" over "we have AI"
3. **time-to-value matters**: if implementation >4 weeks, price goes up
4. **objections are qualifying**: "we already use Excel" → "right, but do you like managing it?"

---

## weekly execution rhythm

1. **outreach**: send 5-10 cold emails (personalized)
2. **discovery calls**: 1-2 scheduled (qualify for tier fit)
3. **follow-up**: 1 proposal sent (based on call notes)
4. **research**: 2-3 new prospects researched (add to database)
